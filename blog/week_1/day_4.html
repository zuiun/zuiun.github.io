Day 4 - Jazz in the Park

This is my blog post for 28 June 2024.

Babeș-Bolyai University#

This time, I actually think it would've been impossible to take a selfie with the billboard. This was on top of a building on the way to UBB, but we all passed it together, so I hope this counts. This billboard, unlike previous advertisements, doesn't have a list of participants. However, it is a competition, and entry is free, which probably makes it more appealing than other music events. Once again, Jazz in the Park has a website.

About Jazz in the Park#

By this point, I have a standard operating procedure for music events. The only languages are Romanian (default) and English, but this is much less egregious of an issue since it doesn't literally take place in a Hungarian castle. This particular festival is a competition, whose top two winners will be invited to perform at the main festival in September. This competition thus features presumably mostly amateur bands - there are no easily recognisable names as in previous festivals, although I admit that I don't know modern jazz that well.

The list of performers is very diverse, and it seems to be all-European. VeszprémFest suggests that there are a lot of Hungarian jazz bands, but the only one here seems to be from Austria. I assume Abel Marton Nagy is a Hungarian living in Austria, which would suggest that ethnicity doesn't matter in music. I can't see an Austrian band performing in Hungarian, while having a Hungarian at the lead of the band is also rather un-Austrian. Jazz is fundamentally a fusion of cultures, so this particular combination seems rather appropriate here.
